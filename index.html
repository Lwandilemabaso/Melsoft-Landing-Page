    Frequently Asked Questions

    Welcome to the FAQ page for our coding boot camp! We hope that this page will help answer any questions you may have about our program.

    1. What is a coding bootcamp?

    A coding bootcamp is a intensive training program that teaches students the skills they need to become proficient in software development. Our bootcamp is designed to give students a solid foundation in web development, including languages such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, as well as frameworks like React and NodeJS.

    2. What kind of support is provided during the program?

    We understand that learning to code can be challenging, so we provide a variety of support resources to help our students succeed. This includes one-on-one time with instructors, weekly progress check-ins, and a dedicated student success team. We also have a strong network of alumni and industry partners who are available to offer guidance and mentorship

    3. What are the entry requirements?

    You must be over 16 and know how to use a mouse and keyboard. The determination and willingness to learn cannot be lacking either

    4. Do I need to know the basics of coding before applying?

    No way! There is no coding experience required to be successful at Melsoft Academy. Globally, coding bootcamp statistics tell us that about 50% of all students (including our Founder!) had no prior programming experience. And there is no evidence that those who already knew how to program do better than those who did not.

    5. Is the program accredited?

    This is a non-accredited program. At the course completion, participants receive a Certificate of Completion, plus a robust portfolio of projects to showcase their skills to potential employers. The Certificate of Completion is not a qualification recognised under the South African Qualifications Framework.
